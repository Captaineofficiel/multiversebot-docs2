MultiverseBot — Rules of Use

Official terms and behavioural rules for using MultiverseBot on Discord. Keep your server friendly — follow these rules.

Last updated: October 19, 2025

officials Rules

  • 1. Be Respectful

    Treat everyone with respect. Harassment, hate speech, threats, and targeted insults are strictly prohibited when interacting with the bot or within servers where the bot is present.

  • 2. No Spam or Abuse

    Do not spam bot commands, mention the bot repeatedly, or otherwise abuse features to disrupt other users or servers. Respect rate limits and command cooldowns.

  • 3. No Illegal or Dangerous Content

    Do not use MultiverseBot to plan, promote, or distribute illegal activity, violent wrongdoing, or any content that facilitates harm.

  • 4. No NSFW or Sexual Content

    Unless explicitly permitted by the bot’s configuration and the server rules, avoid posting sexual or pornographic content through the bot.

  • 5. Respect Privacy

    Do not attempt to extract private or personal data via the bot or publish other people’s private information.

  • 6. Follow Developer & Discord Terms

    By using MultiverseBot, you agree to follow the bot developer’s policies and Discord’s Terms of Service and Community Guidelines.

  • 7. Command & API Use

    Do not reverse-engineer or misuse the bot’s API, tokens, or internal endpoints. For integrations, contact the bot developers for official options.

  • 8. Moderation & Enforcement

    Server admins may restrict or ban users who violate rules. The bot developers reserve the right to remove the bot from servers that facilitate repeated abuse.

  • 9. Reporting Problems

    Report bugs, security issues, or abuse to the bot owner by opening an issue in the GitHub repo or contacting support.

  • 10. Content Ownership & License

    Content you create with MultiverseBot remains yours unless otherwise stated. By using some features, you grant the bot permission to process content for functionality and moderation.

Questions? Contact the bot developer or open a GitHub issue in the project repository.
